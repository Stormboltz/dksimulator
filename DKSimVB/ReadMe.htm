*    Summon Gargoyle: The gargoyle now flies lower to the ground, making it susceptible to melee attacks. This ability now has a fixed duration of 30 seconds and a fixed cost of 60 runic power.
*    Unholy Blight: This talent has been redesigned. It no longer deals damage to nearby targets. Instead, when you deal damage with Death Coil, the target will take periodic damage for 10 seconds equal to 30% of the damage done by Death Coil. This damage accumulates in the same way as Ignite and Deep Wounds.
*    Glyph of Unholy Blight Increases the damage done by Unholy Blight by 40%.
*    Threat of Thassarian: New 3-point talent. When dual-wielding, your Death Strike, Obliterate, Plague Strike, Blood Strike and Frost Strike have a 30/60/100% chance to also deal damage with your off-hand weapon. Off-hand strikes are roughly one half the effect of the original strike.

Priority configuration.

How does it works:

Every available GDC, the simulator will check if the ability or disease is available.

For a Frost/Unholy ability, (e.g. Howling Blast), the simulator is able to wait until it’s available and not use rune for a single use ability.

On a disease case, the simulator will check if it’s not the target or will fade soon. Then if needed, launch the appropriate ability.

If Howling Blast is available and glyphed, it will be use for Frost Fever refresh.

It’s recommended, once you have done a new priority list, to open combat log, to check the cycle that it had generated.

 

To add/edit a Priority configuration file:

Go to the Priority folder.

Copy or edit one of the xml file.

Set your priority. The first in the list is the highest in the priority list and so on.

Reload the simulator.

The runes buffer

Implementation :

From http://elitistjerks.com/f47/t36638-death_knight_pve_dps/ :

It was always assumed that Runes just had a pure 10 second regen time. This has proven to be false from various tests. The behaviour that has been noted is that after the first set of runes very often runes regen after 8 seconds. The current concensus is the following:

Runes have a 10 second regen time. However, if you do not use your regenerated rune as soon as it comes up, it will activate a timer (up to 2 seconds, is the current theory) which will count as the Rune being used. Think of it like this:

Time 

Ability 

Regen time 

Runes 

00:00:00

Icy Touch

 

BBFUU

00:10:00

 

10s

BBFFUU

00:11:50

Icy Touch

 

BBFUU

00:20:00

 

8.5s

BBFFUU



Because you waited 1.5s after the rune was available to use the second frost rune, it's regen time was reduced to 8.5s. Do note, if you wait longer than 2 seconds the above does not apply.
